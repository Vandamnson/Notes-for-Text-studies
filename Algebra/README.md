# Algebra — Dummit & Foote

Independent reading and worked problems through *Abstract Algebra*
(Dummit & Foote, 3rd ed.), Parts II (Ring Theory) and III (Modules &
Vector Spaces). In progress.

## What's in here

**Rings:**
ring axioms and examples; zero divisors and units; integral domains and
fields; Frac(R) construction; principal ideals and the failure of
principality in Z[x]; Euclidean / PID / UFD chain with examples;
Z[i] as Euclidean; the classical proof that Z[(1+√−19)/2] is *not*
Euclidean; structure of (Z/p^n Z)\*; finite multiplicative subgroups of
a field are cyclic; polynomial rings and irreducibility.

**Modules:**
left R-module definition; module homomorphisms; examples (vector spaces
as F-modules, abelian groups as Z-modules, F[x]-modules from a chosen
linear operator); submodules; free modules of finite rank.
